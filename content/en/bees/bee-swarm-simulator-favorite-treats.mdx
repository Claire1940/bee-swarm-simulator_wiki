<Section icon={Map} title="Treat Compatibility & Bond System">

Understanding how treats interact with your hive is the cornerstone of progression. In Bee Swarm Simulator, feeding your bees is the primary method to level them up, which increases their gather amount, attack power, and conversion speed. While basic Treats bought from the dispenser provide a flat 10 bond, specialized treats like Strawberries, Blueberries, Pineapples, and Sunflower Seeds behave differently based on which bee consumes them.

When a bee is fed its designated favorite treat, the bond reward is doubled. If you feed a treat to a bee that does not favor it, the bond is halved to a meager 5 points, and you completely forfeit any chance of triggering a gifted transformation. Therefore, random feeding is highly inefficient and wastes valuable crafting resources.

The table below outlines the core differences in bond yields and transformation capabilities between basic treats and specialized treats.

| **Pineapple** | 5 | 10 | 1 in 8,000 (Non-Mythic) |
| **Treat (Basic)** | 10 | N/A | Cannot trigger transformation |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Gifted Transformation Odds & Costs">

Turning your bees into **Gifted Bees** is essential for unlocking powerful hive-wide passive bonuses. While Star Treats offer a guaranteed transformation, using **bee swarm simulator favorite treats** is the most common method for non-event bees. However, this process relies heavily on random number generation (RNG).

The chance of a bee becoming gifted from a single favorite treat depends entirely on its rarity. Because the odds are quite low, you must prepare to feed thousands of treats in a single session to secure a transformation.

### Gifted Transformation Probabilities

The following data represents the average statistical requirements for transforming bees using their favorite treats.

| Bee Rarity | Base Gifted Chance | Average Treats Required | Estimated Honey Cost (Equivalent) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rare Bees** | 1 in 8,000 | 8,000 treats | 40,000,000 Honey |
| **Epic Bees** | 1 in 8,000 | 8,000 treats | 40,000,000 Honey |
| **Legendary Bees** | 1 in 8,000 | 8,000 treats | 40,000,000 Honey |
| **Mythic Bees** | 1 in 24,000 | 24,000 treats | 120,000,000 Honey |
| **Event Bees** | N/A | Impossible via treats | Requires Star Treat |

Because these numbers are statistical averages, you might get lucky and transform a bee in under 1,000 treats, or you might find yourself empty-handed after spending 20,000. For Mythic bees, the 1 in 24,000 odds make treat-based transformation a late-game endeavor.

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning: Crafting vs. Feeding Dilemma">
Before dumping thousands of Strawberries, Blueberries, or Pineapples into your hive, remember that these items are key ingredients for crafting high-tier gear. For example, Blue Extracts require Blueberries, and Enzymes require Pineapples. Do not leave yourself unable to craft your next major gear upgrade because you chased a gifted roll too early.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Feeding Step-by-Step">

To minimize resource waste and maximize your chances of success, you should follow a structured feeding protocol. Blindly clicking the feed button can result in over-leveling bees you plan to replace or wasting rare treats on the wrong targets.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify Your Target Bee">
    Analyze your hive and select a single bee that needs a gifted bonus or a level upgrade. Prioritize bees that offer universally beneficial hive bonuses, such as Commander Bee (+5% Critical Chance) or Music Bee (+10% Critical Power).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stockpile a Safe Margin of Treats">
    Do not attempt a gifted transformation with only 1,000 treats. For regular bees, wait until you have at least 10,000 of their favorite treat. For Mythic bees, do not start until you have accumulated at least 25,000 to 30,000 treats to combat bad RNG.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Your Feeding Settings">
    Open your hive menu, click on the target bee, and select the appropriate treat. Use the batch slider or select the "Until Gifted" option if you have a massive stockpile. This prevents manual clicking and stops feeding immediately once the bee transforms.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Re-evaluate Hive Synergy">
    Once a bee becomes gifted, its hive bonus is active. Because identical gifted bonuses do not stack, immediately identify any duplicate gifted bees in your hive and prepare to replace them with other unique types to diversify your bonuses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success: Gifted Hive Bonus Activation">
Once a bee transforms, its unique hive bonus applies to your entire character instantly. Always prioritize keeping one of each gifted Rare, Epic, and Legendary bee in your hive before aiming for pure color specialization.
</Callout>
