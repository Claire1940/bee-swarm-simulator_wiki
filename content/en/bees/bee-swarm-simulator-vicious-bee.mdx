<Section icon={Map} title="Bee Swarm Simulator Vicious Bee Spawn Locations">

Finding a wild Vicious Bee requires a solid understanding of the game's day and night cycles. Wild versions of this bee do not wander freely; instead, they spawn as stationary shadow spikes hidden in specific fields exclusively during the night phase. The day phase in the game lasts for 14 minutes, followed by a 6-minute night phase. 

As soon as night falls, there is a fixed chance for a stinger spike to emerge from the ground. To initiate the boss fight, you must physically walk your character directly into the protruding stinger. The spike can spawn in one of five designated fields, depending on your progression and unlocked zones.

| Field Name | Gate Requirement | Spawn Probability | Terrain Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clover Field** | 0 Bees | Low | Flat, easy to navigate |
| **Spider Field** | 5 Bees | Medium | Spider spawns nearby |
| **Cactus Field** | 15 Bees | Medium | High elevation obstacles |
| **Rose Field** | 15 Bees | High | Red field flowers |
| **Mountain Top** | 25 Bees | Very High | Dangerous environmental hazards |

To farm stingers efficiently, you should establish a reliable scouting route. As soon as the night cycle begins, use the red cannon or yellow launcher to gain altitude, then scan the Clover, Spider, Cactus, Rose, and Mountain Top fields from above. Look for a dark, glowing spike sticking out of the soil.

<Callout type="tip" title="Night Cycle Spawning Tip">
Listen closely when night falls. A distinct, low-pitched chime plays globally across the map if a wild Vicious Bee spike has spawned in one of the active fields. If you hear this sound, drop everything and begin scouting.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Strategy: How to Defeat Wild Vicious Bee">

Defeating a wild Vicious Bee requires careful movement and preparation. The wild bee's level is determined by the highest-level bee in your current hive. For example, if your strongest bee is level 8, the wild Vicious Bee will spawn at level 8. This scaling mechanic ensures that the fight remains challenging throughout your entire playthrough.

During the battle, the wild bee will hover over the field and periodically summon ground spikes, marked by dark circular shadows. Standing inside these shadows when the spike emerges deals massive damage to your character and can easily result in a sudden defeat.

You cannot hatch the Vicious Bee from a standard egg or obtain it through Royal Jelly rolls. Instead, you must purchase its egg from the Shadow Outpost using Stingers. This requires a significant grind, making Stinger management a top priority during the early-to-mid transition of your gameplay.

Follow these steps to successfully collect the required resources and claim your own Vicious Bee:

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Locate the Shadow Outpost">
    Climb the ramp near the Rose Field and head behind the 20-Bee gate area to find the hidden black Stinger pedestal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Purchase the Vicious Bee Egg">
    Interact with the shadow pedestal to exchange your 250 accumulated Stingers for the Vicious Bee Egg.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Hatch and Position the Bee">
    Place the egg into an empty slot in your hive. Position it in the bottom rows to ensure its combat tokens drop close to your character.
  </Step>

Once you have hatched the bee, your next major goal should be turning it into a Gifted Bee. A Gifted Vicious Bee provides one of the most powerful passive bonuses in the entire game: a permanent 15 percent reduction in monster respawn times. This bonus drastically accelerates your material farming routes by allowing you to defeat mobs like the Werewolf, Spider, and Ladybugs much more frequently.

To upgrade your bee, feed it a Star Treat. This is the most reliable method, as Event Bees do not have favorite treats and cannot be turned gifted through standard food items.
