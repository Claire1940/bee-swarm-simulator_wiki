<Section icon={Map} title="Core Mechanics: Your Bee Swarm Simulator Beginner Guide">

Entering the world of Bee Swarm Simulator can feel overwhelming with dozens of fields, hundreds of bee types, and a mountain of gear. The fundamental gameplay loop is simple: walk into a field to collect pollen using your tool, return to your hive to convert that pollen into honey, and spend that honey on more bees and better equipment. As you grow your swarm, you will pass through gates that require a specific number of bees, unlocking higher fields and more complex mechanics.

- **The First Session**: How to claim your hive and start your first pollen collection loop.

To maximize your efficiency during the first few hours, you need to understand your hive's stats. Every bee has a specific gathering speed and attack power. Balancing these is key to handling both peaceful farming and the occasional bug fight.

| Stat | Description | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pollen Capacity** | Total pollen your bag can hold | **High** - Limits your farm time |
| **Convert Rate** | How fast bees turn pollen to honey | **Medium** - Reduces downtime at hive |
| **Bee Gather** | Amount of pollen bees collect directly | **High** - Main source of income |
| **Attack Power** | Damage dealt to bugs and bosses | **Low** - Only for combat fields |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression Ladder: Gear and Tool Upgrade Order">

Success in the early game depends on spending your honey wisely. It is tempting to buy every shiny new tool you see, but following a structured upgrade path prevents resource waste. In the beginning, your primary goal is to reach the 10-bee gate and then the 25-bee gate to access the Pro Shop and Mountain Top Shop.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Starter Tools">
    Focus on the **Vacuum** or **Wood Scythe**. These provide a broad reach for early fields like Sunflower and Dandelion.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Container Upgrades">
    Move from the Pouch to the **Backpack** quickly. You cannot make honey if you are constantly running back to the hive.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Utility Items">
    Invest in **Basic Eggs** to fill your first 10 slots. Do not worry about rarity yet; quantity is better than quality early on.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Once you move past the Noob Shop, the gear costs increase significantly. Use the following table to plan your purchases in the Pro Shop.

| Item | Cost (Honey) | Primary Benefit | Recommendation |

<Callout type="warning" title="Royal Jelly Trap">

<Section icon={Target} title="Quest Mastery: Bear NPC Walkthrough">

Quests are the roadmap of Bee Swarm Simulator. Each Bear NPC focuses on a different aspect of the game. Following their tasks ensures you visit every field and learn the mechanics of various items like Gumdrops, Stingers, and Moon Charms.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Black Bear's Pollen Missions">
    Black Bear provides the most straightforward quests. He will ask you to collect specific amounts of pollen from different fields. Completing his lines often rewards you with Silver, Gold, and even Diamond Eggs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mother Bear's Social Tasks">
    Mother Bear teaches you how to raise your bees. Her quests involve feeding treats to bees and leveling them up. This is the fastest way to earn Star Jellies, which turn a bee into a Gifted version.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Science Bear's Technical Quests">
    Science Bear is located up the ramp near the Red Drive. His quests are harder but reward you with **Translators**, allowing you to speak to Stick Bug, Riley Bee, and Bucko Bee.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Panda Bear's Combat Trials">
    If you enjoy fighting bugs, Panda Bear is your go-to. His quests require defeating Spiders, Werewolves, and Ladybugs. He rewards you with Ant Passes and eggs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Bear NPC | Difficulty | Main Reward Type | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Black Bear** | Easy | Honey and Eggs | **Very High** |
| **Mother Bear** | Easy | Treats and Star Jellies | **High** |
| **Science Bear** | Medium | Translators and Ability Buffs | **High** |
| **Panda Bear** | Medium | Combat rewards and Ant Passes | **Medium** |
| **Spirit Bear** | Hard | Spirit Petals and Endgame items | **Low (Late Game)** |

<Callout type="success" title="Translator Tip">
When you get your first Translator from Science Bear, most players recommend giving it to **Riley Bee** (Red) or **Bucko Bee** (Blue) to start earning specialized rewards and faction reputation.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Systems: Amulets and Field Management">

As you progress toward 30 bees, you will encounter Amulets. These are permanent equipment pieces that provide massive stat boosts. You don't "buy" these; you earn them by completing challenges or defeating bosses.

- **Ant Amulet**: Earned from the Ant Challenge. Focus on high "Pollen" and "Bee Attack" stats.
- **Moon Amulet**: Created using Moon Charms. It provides a huge boost to your capacity.
- **King Beetle Amulet**: A rare drop from the King Beetle boss in the Blue Flower Field.
- **Star Amulet**: Unlocked based on the number of **unique** Gifted bees in your hive.

| Amulet Type | How to Get | Best Stat to Look For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Moon Amulet** | 100 Moon Charms | +Capacity and +White Pollen |
| **Ant Amulet** | Ant Challenge Score | +Critical Chance and +Pollen |
| **Star Amulet** | Gifted Bee Milestones | +Convert Rate and +Critical Power |

<Callout type="tip" title="Field Boosts">
Use the **Field Boosters** (located in the Blue and Red HQ) every time they are off cooldown. They provide a 2x or 3x multiplier to specific fields for 15 minutes, allowing you to finish difficult quests in record time.
</Callout>

</Section>
