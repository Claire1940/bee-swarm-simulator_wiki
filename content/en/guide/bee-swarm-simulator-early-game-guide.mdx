<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Early Progression Guide">

To progress quickly through the early stages, you should follow a structured path. Randomly buying items or hatching eggs without a plan can lead to "bottlenecks" where you have plenty of honey but no bag space, or a huge bag but a very slow collection tool. This **bee swarm simulator early game guide** recommends the following sequence to maximize your efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Core Loop">
    Claim an empty hive and hatch your first Basic Bee. Immediately head to the Sunflower or Dandelion fields. Focus on short trips: fill your bag, return to the hive, and convert. Repeat this until you can afford your first 5 bees.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Prioritize Container Upgrades">
    In the early game, your bag capacity is usually the biggest bottleneck. Purchase the **Pouch** and then the **Backpack** as soon as possible. Being able to stay in the field longer means more ability tokens from your bees, which accelerates honey making.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlock the 10-Bee Gate">
    Once you have 10 bees, you gain access to the Pro Shop. This area contains significantly better tools. Continue completing Black Bear quests, as they provide the honey needed to reach this milestone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Focus on Mother Bear Quests">
    Mother Bear teaches you about bee levels and bonding. Completing her tasks is crucial because she rewards you with **Star Treats** and **Royal Jellies**, which are essential for turning your basic bees into Gifted or Rare variants.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Road to 25 Bees">
    The 25-Bee Gate is the gateway to the "Mid-Game." This unlocks the Mountain Top Shop, where you will find the Porcelain Dipper—the most important tool for any player transitioning out of the early game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Ability Token Tip">
Always prioritize picking up tokens dropped by your bees. Tokens like **Focus** (increases critical power) and **Haste** (increases movement speed) are vital for maintaining a high collection rate in the field.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Early Game Bee Tier List & Hive Composition">

In the early game, you should not worry about "hive colors" (Red, Blue, or White). Instead, aim for a **Mixed Hive**. A mixed hive allows you to complete any quest regardless of which field the Bear NPCs send you to. Some bees are significantly better than others for beginners because they provide universal buffs.
