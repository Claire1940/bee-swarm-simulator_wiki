When you first spawn into the game, your immediate objective is to claim an empty hive and start gathering pollen from the low-level fields near the spawn area, such as the Sunflower Field, Dandelion Field, and Clover Field. Early progression is built entirely around a simple loop: collect pollen until your bag is full, return to your hive to convert that pollen into honey, and spend that honey on more bees and better equipment. 

In this early stage, do not waste your honey on cosmetic items or random Royal Jelly rolls. Every drop of honey should be funneled directly into expanding your hive slots and upgrading your basic gear.

<Section icon={Target} title="The Bee Swarm Simulator Noob to Pro Mid-Game Strategy">

Transitioning from a beginner to a mid-game player requires reaching the 15-bee and 25-bee gates. These gates unlock higher areas of the mountain, granting access to more lucrative fields, stronger monsters, and advanced shops. The **bee swarm simulator noob to pro** transition truly begins here, where you must transition from buying basic tools to saving up for the legendary Mountain Top Shop gear.

Your main goal in the mid-game is to obtain the Porcelain Dipper and the Porcelain Port-O-Hive. These two items will drastically increase your pollen collection rates and capacity, allowing you to stay in fields longer and complete demanding quests.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Hive Specialization: Choosing Your Endgame Color">

However, switching colors too early is a major mistake. You should only specialize after you have unlocked the Supreme Star Amulet (SSA), which requires discovering 40 unique gifted bee types.
