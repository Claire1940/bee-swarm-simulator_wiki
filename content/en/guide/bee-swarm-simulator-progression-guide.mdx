<Section icon={Map} title="Early Game Starter Phase and Core Mechanics">

Navigating the early stages of Roblox Bee Swarm Simulator requires a strong understanding of the core loop: collecting pollen, converting it into honey at your hive, and hatching new bees to expand your swarm. In the beginning, your primary goal is to unlock new areas of the mountain by growing your bee count. Do not rush to buy expensive cosmetic items or spend rare resources early on. Instead, focus on establishing a highly efficient farming routine.

This **bee swarm simulator progression guide** outlines the exact steps you need to follow to build a solid foundation. Joining the official Roblox group and keeping track of active codes can give you a massive boost in honey and tickets, which are vital for purchasing your first set of eggs and basic tools.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim Your Hive">
    Find an empty hive slot at the base of the mountain and claim it to begin your journey.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Collect Starter Pollen">
    Equip your basic scoop and head to the early fields like Sunflower, Dandelion, or Clover to collect pollen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Convert Pollen to Honey">
    Return to your hive when your backpack is full. Stand near your bees to convert your pollen into honey.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Hatch Eggs and Expand">
    Use your newly earned honey to purchase basic eggs. Place them into empty slots to increase your active bee count.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Mid-Game Mountain Climb and Gear Purchase Order">

Once you reach 25 bees, the mountain top area becomes your main hub. This phase is all about optimizing your gear purchase order. The Mountain Top Shop contains several high-tier items that will dramatically increase your collection speed and backpack capacity. Many players make the mistake of buying items out of order, which slows down their progression.

The absolute priority in this phase is saving up for the Porcelain Dipper, followed by the Porcelain Port-O-Hive. These two items represent a massive power spike. While saving up the required honey can feel like a grind, avoiding temporary or inefficient tool upgrades will save you hours of farming in the long run.

| Purchase Priority | Equipment Name | Primary Benefit | Progression Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Priority 1** | Belt Bag / Starter Belt | Increases early pollen capacity | Essential for reducing hive trips |
| **Priority 2** | Golden Rake | Collects pollen from multiple flowers | Great transitional tool before Porcelain |
| **Priority 3** | Porcelain Dipper | Elite pollen harvesting tool | The most important mid-game weapon |
| **Priority 4** | Porcelain Port-O-Hive | Massive mid-game backpack capacity | Allows for much longer farming sessions |

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Never spend rare materials like Glitter, Enzymes, Glue, or Oil on early-game shortcuts. These items are incredibly difficult to farm later and are required in massive quantities for late-game crafted gear.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crafted Gear and Mid-Game Mask Selection">

As you progress toward 35 bees, you will move away from standard shop purchases and transition into crafted gear. The Blender and the various crafting stations around the map allow you to combine basic ingredients into powerful equipment. During this phase, choosing the right mask is your most important decision. 

There are three main mid-game masks available: the Honey Mask, the Bubble Mask, and the Fire Mask. Each mask caters to a specific playstyle, but they also offer unique passives that can completely change how you farm.

  <Card title="Bubble Mask">

  <Card title="Fire Mask">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Late-Game Hive Specialization and Colors">

Reaching 45 bees and unlocking the Supreme Star Amulet (SSA) marks the beginning of the late game. This is the stage where you should finally choose a specialized hive color: Blue, Red, or White. Choosing a color too early is a common mistake that can severely hinder your progress, as early-game quests require you to collect pollen of all colors.

Each hive color has a distinct playstyle, gear requirement, and difficulty level. Blue hives are generally the most affordable and beginner-friendly option for late-game players, while Red and White hives require massive investments of both honey and rare crafting materials to become viable.

<Section icon={Target} title="Bear Quest Priorities and Key System Unlocks">

Quests are the backbone of progression in Bee Swarm Simulator. The various Bear NPCs scattered around the mountain offer unique questlines that reward you with honey, eggs, tickets, and crucial system unlocks. Prioritizing the right quests ensures that you unlock essential gameplay mechanics as quickly as possible.

In particular, Science Bear's quests should be prioritized because they reward you with Translators. These Translators allow you to speak with specialized NPCs like Riley Bee and Bucko Bee, unlocking further questlines and rewards. Later on, Spirit Bear's quests will reward you with Spirit Petals, which are used to craft some of the best gear in the game.

| Quest Giver | Primary Focus | Major Progression Reward | Strategy Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Black Bear** | General pollen collection | Mythic Egg & steady honey | Complete these continuously as you farm |
| **Mother Bear** | Bee leveling and bonding | Star Jelly & treats | Focus on this early to boost your hive level |
| **Science Bear** | Field research and mechanics | Translators & Science Enhancements | High priority; unlocks communication with key NPCs |
| **Spirit Bear** | Advanced, multi-field tasks | Spirit Petals | Late-game priority; critical for Petal Wand |

<Callout type="success" title="Spirit Petal Priority">
When you obtain your first Spirit Petal from Spirit Bear, use it to craft the Petal Wand. This tool automatically harvests pollen and converts a portion of it, making your manual farming sessions significantly more efficient.
</Callout>
