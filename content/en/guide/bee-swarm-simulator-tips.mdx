<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering the Early Game Progression">

Starting your journey in Bee Swarm Simulator requires a disciplined approach to resource management. The core gameplay loop involves gathering pollen from various flower fields, returning to your hive to convert that pollen into honey, and using that honey to purchase more bees and better equipment. In the beginning, your primary goal is to reach 25 bees as quickly as possible to unlock the higher mountain areas.

Early on, players often make the mistake of wandering into high-level fields without enough hive power. Stick to the starter fields like the Sunflower Field or Dandelion Field until you have at least 10 bees. This ensures you aren't spending too much time traveling back and forth with a small capacity bag.

| **Pouch** | 400 | Increases pollen capacity | High |

| **Scooper** | 1,200 | Better flower coverage | Medium |
| **Magnet** | 2,500 | Collects tokens from distance | **Very High** |
| **Backpack** | 12,000 | Massive capacity boost | High |

<Callout type="warning" title="Bag Management">
Never let your bag stay at 100% capacity while farming. Every second spent standing in a field with a full bag is wasted honey. If your bag fills in less than 60 seconds, prioritize upgrading your container immediately.
</Callout>

As you progress toward 20 bees, you will encounter the Pro Shop. This is where your gear starts to have a significant impact on your farming speed. Focus on tools that offer "pollen from all flowers" rather than color-specific tools until you are ready to specialize your hive in the late game.

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Gear Order and Milestones">

Progression in Bee Swarm Simulator is often measured by the gear you carry. Purchasing the wrong item at the wrong time can stall your progress for days. Following a strict upgrade path ensures you always have the collection power necessary to complete the increasingly difficult quests from Science Bear and Spirit Bear.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Basic Hive Phase">
    Focus on reaching 25 bees. Use the Mountain Top Shop as your primary goal. Purchase the **Golden Rake** as it is the most efficient tool for mixed-field farming in the early stages.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Porcelain Milestone">
    The **Porcelain Dipper** and **Porcelain Port-O-Hive** are your most important mid-game acquisitions. These items allow you to farm millions of honey per minute and are required to handle the pollen requirements of Black Bear's later quests.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Crafted Masks and Guards">
    Once you have Porcelain gear, move to the Ace Shop. You should aim for the **Honey Mask** first. Its "Coin Scatter" ability transforms full bags into honey marks, allowing you to stay in the field longer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Spirit Petal Progression">
    Complete Spirit Bear's 10th quest to get your first Spirit Petal. Use this to craft the **Petal Wand**. This tool automatically converts a portion of gathered pollen into honey, drastically reducing hive trips.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Spirit Petal Priority">
Most veteran players recommend using your first Spirit Petal for the **Petal Wand**, the second for the **Windy Bee** (via the Wind Shrine), and the third for the **Petal Belt**. This order provides the most consistent progression curve.
</Callout>

Beyond the Petal Wand, your focus shifts to the "Endgame" gear located in the Coconut Shop and the specialized color shops. The **Coconut Canister** is a mandatory upgrade for any serious player, providing an emergency shield and massive capacity.

</Section>
