Building a **bee swarm simulator mixed hive** is the most efficient way to progress through the game's complex quest system. Unlike specialized Red or Blue hives, a mixed hive maintains a balanced ratio of bees that can collect pollen effectively from any field. This versatility is crucial because NPCs like Black Bear, Science Bear, and Spirit Bear frequently assign tasks that require massive amounts of pollen from diverse locations across the mountain.

In 2026, the meta for mixed hives focuses on maximizing "Gifted" bonuses and maintaining high token uptime. By not tethering yourself to a single color, you ensure that every field boost you receive—whether from a Field Dice or a specialized booster—provides tangible progress toward your current goals.

In 2026, the priority for a mixed hive remains focused on Event Bees and high-utility Gifted bees. Event bees are unique because they do not have a color bias that hurts your overall performance.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression Path and Gear Priority">

Your progression in a **bee swarm simulator mixed hive** is measured by your gear as much as your bees. Because you are farming all fields, you need "neutral" gear that doesn't over-index on a single color. The Mountain Top Shop is your primary destination for mid-game upgrades.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Porcelain Era">
    Focus entirely on obtaining the **Porcelain Dipper** and **Porcelain Port-O-Hive**. These are the gold standard for mixed players. Avoid the Red/Blue collectors in the 10-bee and 15-bee zones unless you are struggling with a specific quest.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Mask Choice">
    While the **Honey Mask** is the traditional choice for mixed hives due to "Coin Scatter," in 2026, many players also utilize the **Fire or Bubble masks** temporarily to clear difficult color-specific quests from Science Bear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Spirit Petal Journey">
    Once you reach the 35-bee zone, your life revolves around Spirit Bear. Your first Spirit Petal should almost always go toward the **Petal Wand**. Its ability to convert pollen into honey while you swing is a game-changer for long farming sessions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Amulet Grind">
    Consistently defeat the King Beetle, Tunnel Bear, and Stump Snail. You need to upgrade your **Star Amulet** as frequently as possible to increase your capacity and pollen multipliers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Gear Item | Cost | Requirement | Mixed Hive Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Porcelain Dipper** | 150M Honey | Mountain Top Shop | Best all-around collection tool |
| **Honey Mask** | 100M Honey + Materials | Ace Shop | Coin Scatter converts pollen instantly |
| **Honeycomb Belt** | 150M Honey + Materials | Ace Shop | Massive capacity and loot luck |
| **Petal Wand** | 1.5B Honey + 1 Petal | Spirit Bear | Auto-conversion and long-range reach |

<Callout type="warning" title="Material Management">
Do not spend your **Glitter, Tropical Drinks, or Enzymes** casually. These are extremely hard to craft in the late game and are required for the "Endgame Masks" (Demon, Diamond, Gummy). Save them while you are a mixed hive!
</Callout>

</Section>
