<Section icon={Map} title="Starter to Early Mountain Gear Progression">

The initial phase of your journey in Bee Swarm Simulator is defined by the "Collect-Convert-Upgrade" loop. In this stage, your primary goal is to reach the 25-bee gate to access the Mountain Top Shop. The **bee swarm simulator equipment guide** for beginners emphasizes frequent trips to the hive; do not over-farm in the fields if your bag is full, as uncollected pollen is wasted potential.

During the first 10-15 bees, your equipment choices are relatively linear. You should aim to upgrade your basic scooper and pouch as soon as you have the honey. The goal is efficiency: if you find yourself standing at your hive for more than 30 seconds to convert, you need a better bag. If it takes you too long to fill that bag, you need a better tool.

| Starter Item | Category | Honey Cost | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Skillet** | Tool | 8,000 | Faster early field clearing |
| **Jar** | Container | 12,000 | Increased early storage |
| **Clipper** | Tool | 25,000 | Better for flower-specific collection |
| **Pouch** | Container | 45,000 | Essential for 10-bee zone farming |
| **Magnet** | Tool | 150,000 | Pulls tokens from a distance |

<Callout type="tip" title="Beginner Efficiency Tip">
Do not spend your tickets on Gold Eggs or basic resources early on. Save every ticket for the **Tabby Bee** or **Photon Bee**, as these event bees provide better "equipment" value through their permanent abilities than any early-game tool.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Mountain Top Shop Milestones">

Once you pass the 25-bee gate, you enter the "Mid-Early" game. This is where the **bee swarm simulator equipment guide** becomes more strategic. The Mountain Top Shop contains the most iconic mid-game gear: the Porcelain set. These items are expensive but represent a massive leap in honey-making capability.

The **Porcelain Dipper** is arguably the most important tool you will buy before reaching the late-game crafting stage. It collects significantly more pollen and has a unique ability to create a "pollen wave" that clears large sections of a field. Following this, the **Porcelain Port-O-Hive** provides the capacity needed to stay in high-tier fields like the Pineapple Patch or Cactus Field for extended periods.

| Mountain Top Gear | Type | Honey Cost | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Porcelain Dipper** | Tool | 150,000,000 | High collection + Pollen Wave |
| **Porcelain Port-O-Hive** | Container | 250,000,000 | 500,000+ Base Capacity |
| **Beekeeper's Mask** | Mask | 20,000,000 | Increased honey from tokens |
| **Beekeeper's Boots** | Shoes | 15,000,000 | Movement speed and jump power |
| **Mondo Belt Bag** | Belt | 10,000,000 | Extra loot and capacity |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid the 'Shiny' Trap">
Many players waste honey on the Sparkstaff. While it looks impressive, the **Porcelain Dipper** is vastly superior for overall progression. Save your 150 million honey for the Dipper rather than side-grading to the Sparkstaff.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Mid-Game Crafting and Mask Selection">

As you approach 35 bees, the game shifts from pure honey purchases to material-heavy crafting. This is the "Mid-Game" wall where you must balance your honey production with the collection of Glitter, Oil, Enzymes, and Extracts. The **bee swarm simulator equipment guide** at this stage revolves around the Ace Shop and the specialized masks.

You will need to choose a mask that fits your current hive composition. While most players should stay "Mixed" until they get their Supreme Star Amulet (SSA), the **Honey Mask** is widely considered the best all-around mid-game mask due to its "Coin Scatter" ability, which converts pollen into honey instantly in the field.

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Unlock the Petal Wand">

The Petal Wand is a legendary tool that marks your transition into the late game. It requires a grueling questline and a significant amount of rare materials. Follow these steps to ensure you are prepared for the craft.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete Spirit Bear's 10th Quest">
    You must finish the first 10 quests from Spirit Bear to receive your first Spirit Petal. These quests require billions of pollen from various fields.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stockpile Crafting Materials">
    You will need 1.5 Billion Honey, 1 Spirit Petal, 10 Star Jellies, 25 Glitter, and 75 Enzymes. Start saving your Enzymes early, as they are often the biggest bottleneck.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Craft at the Spirit Shrine">
    Once you have the materials, head to the Spirit Shrine located in the 35-bee zone to claim your wand.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Your Hive">
    The Petal Wand benefits from high attack speed and conversion buffs. Ensure your hive has a few **Music Bees** for the Melody buff to maximize the wand's effectiveness.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="The Petal Belt Debate">
While the Petal Wand is the standard first choice, some advanced players opt for the **Petal Belt** if they already have a high-level hive and want more capacity. However, for 95% of players, the Wand is the superior first pick.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Endgame Gear and Hive Specialization">

The final tier of the **bee swarm simulator equipment guide** covers the endgame collectors and the "Big Three" masks: Diamond, Demon, and Gummy. At this stage, you are no longer a mixed hive. You have likely unlocked your Supreme Star Amulet and chosen a color: Red, Blue, or White.

Endgame gear is astronomically expensive, requiring trillions of honey and thousands of materials. These items are designed to synergize with specific bee types and field colors, allowing for honey gains in the hundreds of billions per hour.

| Endgame Tool | Hive Color | Requirement | Unique Power |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tide Popper** | Blue | 2.5T Honey + Blue Materials | Creates tidal waves for massive blue pollen |
| **Dark Scythe** | Red | 2.5T Honey + Red Materials | Spawns dark flames and boosts red crit |
| **Gummyballer** | White | 5T Honey + Goo Materials | Infinite goo and massive white pollen scaling |
| **Diamond Mask** | Blue | 5 Diamond Eggs + Blue Mats | 3x Blue Capacity and Diamond Drain |
| **Demon Mask** | Red | 5 Stingers + Red Mats | High combat damage and Flame Heat |
| **Gummy Mask** | White | 250 Gumdrops + Goo Mats | Gummy Morph and massive goo collection |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Diamond Egg Rule">
**Never use your Diamond Eggs!** You need 5 of them to craft the Diamond Mask. If you hatch them early in the game, you will be forced to spend thousands of tickets or Robux to get them back later. This is the most common mistake in the Bee Swarm Simulator equipment guide.
</Callout>
