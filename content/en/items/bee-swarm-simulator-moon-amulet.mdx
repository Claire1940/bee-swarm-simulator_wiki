The **Bee Swarm Simulator moon amulet** is one of the earliest and most impactful amulets a player can obtain. Unlike the Ant or Star amulets, which require combat or gifted bee milestones, the Moon Amulet is earned through resource collection. To generate one, you must visit the Moon Amulet Generator located in the Pineapple Patch area. Each generation costs **100 Moon Charms**.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression and Capacity Scaling">

Understanding the progression of the **Bee Swarm Simulator moon amulet** is essential for inventory management. Many players mistakenly stop rolling after their 10th amulet, thinking they have reached the "max." While the capacity bonus stops increasing at +250,000, the "Quality" of the amulet continues to scale until you have generated 40 amulets in total.

Higher quality increases the probability of rolling higher percentage values for secondary stats like Bond from Treats, Honey Per Pollen, and Critical Power. According to the [Bee Swarm Simulator Wiki](https://bee-swarm-simulator.fandom.com/wiki/Moon_Amulet), the capacity increases by 25,000 per roll, with a slight exception at the 7th roll.

| Generation Number | Capacity Bonus | Quality Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1st Amulet | +25,000 | 0.025 |
| 5th Amulet | +125,000 | 0.125 |
| 10th Amulet | +250,000 (MAX) | 0.250 |
| 25th Amulet | +250,000 | 0.625 |
| 40th Amulet | +250,000 | 1.000 (MAX) |

<Callout type="warning" title="The 10-Roll Rule">
Do not stress about specific stats until you have reached your 10th roll. Your primary goal in the early game is simply to increase your bag capacity so you can stay in the fields longer. Once you hit the 250,000 cap, you can start being selective about the secondary buffs.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Farming Moon Charms Fast">

To reach the 40-generation cap for your **Bee Swarm Simulator moon amulet**, you will need a total of 4,000 Moon Charms. While this sounds daunting, several methods can speed up the process significantly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Chase Fireflies">
    During the night cycle, Fireflies spawn in various fields. Running through them yields Moon Charms and Glitter. This is the most consistent free method.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Moon Sprouts">
    Planting a Sprout at night has a high chance of becoming a Moon Sprout. These drop large quantities of Moon Charms upon bursting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Memory Match">
    The 15-Bee Zone and 30-Bee Zone Memory Match games frequently offer Moon Charms as rewards. Always prioritize picking them over basic treats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="King Beetle & Mobs">
    Defeating the King Beetle and higher-level mobs like the Coconut Crab can occasionally drop Moon Charms as bonus loot.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Night Bells">
If you are in a rush, using a **Night Bell** will immediately trigger the night cycle, allowing you to use the Moon Amulet Generator and spawn Fireflies instantly.
</Callout>

</Section>
