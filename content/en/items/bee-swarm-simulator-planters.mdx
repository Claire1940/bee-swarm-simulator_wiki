<Section icon={Map} title="Overview of Planters and Nectar Mechanics">

In Bee Swarm Simulator, planters are deployable items that grow over time when placed in various fields. As you collect pollen in the same field, the growth rate of your active planter increases significantly. Once a planter reaches full maturity, harvesting it yields a combination of honey, crafting ingredients, and essential nectars. 

Nectars are temporary, stacking buffs that provide massive multipliers to your hive. There are five distinct types of nectars, each associated with specific fields and gameplay boosts. Managing these nectars is critical for mid-game and late-game progression, as they directly impact how much honey you can make during active boosting sessions.

| Nectar Type | Core Multipliers | Primary Source Fields |
|-|-|-|
| **Refreshing** | Blue Pollen, Capacity, Haste | Strawberry, Blue Flower, Pineapple, Clover |
| **Satisfying** | White Pollen, Honey at Hive, Convert Rate | Sunflower, Pumpkin, Pineapple, Coconut |
| **Comforting** | Defense, Capacity, Convert Rate | Dandelion, Bamboo, Pine Tree |
| **Motivating** | Player Movespeed, Critical Power | Mushroom, Spider, Rose, Stump |
| **Invigorating** | Red Pollen, Bee Attack, Critical Chance | Clover, Cactus, Pepper Patch, Mountain Top |

Each nectar buff can stack up to 24 hours of duration. To maintain these buffs, you must store your harvested nectars in the Nectar Condenser located near the 30-Bee Zone. The condenser allows you to combine and preserve your buffs, ensuring you do not lose progress when transitioning between farming sessions.

<Callout type="info" title="Nectar Decay Warning">
Nectars decay passively over time even when you are offline. To prevent your hard-earned multipliers from disappearing, make it a habit to harvest your planters right before logging off and store the nectars in the condenser.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Field Placement and Farming Routes">

To get the most out of your bee swarm simulator planters, you must match them with their preferred fields. Placing a planter in a preferred field grants a multiplier to its growth speed, increases the nectar harvested, and unlocks unique bonus loot pools. 

| Planter | Best Fields | Guaranteed Bonus Loot |
|-|-|-|
| **Candy** | Strawberry, Pumpkin, Pineapple | Glue, Enzymes, Strawberries |
| **Blue Clay** | Pine Tree Forest, Bamboo Field | Blueberries, Royal Jelly, Blue Extract |
| **Red Balloon** | Pepper Patch, Rose Field, Strawberry | Strawberries, Red Extract, Soft Wax |
| **Tacky** | Sunflower Field, Dandelion Field | Sunflower Seeds, Soft Wax, Bitterberries |
| **Pesticide** | Spider Field, Bamboo Field | Enzymes, Glitter, Neonberries |
| **Petal** | Coconut Field, Clover Field | Glitter, Star Jelly, Enzymes |

Using this placement data, you can establish highly efficient farming routes depending on your current hive specialization.

### Mixed Hive Farming Route
If you are running a mixed hive, your goal is to maintain a balanced distribution of all five nectars to support general questing.
1. Place the **Candy Planter** in the **Strawberry Field** to gather Refreshing Nectar and Glue.
2. Place the **Tacky Planter** in the **Sunflower Field** to gather Satisfying Nectar and Soft Wax.
3. Place the **Pesticide Planter** in the **Spider Field** to gather Motivating Nectar and Neonberries.

### Blue Hive Farming Route
Blue hives require maximum Refreshing and Comforting Nectars to scale blue pollen capacity and bubble generation.
1. Place the **Blue Clay Planter** in the **Pine Tree Forest** to gather Comforting Nectar.
2. Place the **Hydroponic Planter** in the **Bamboo Field** to maintain a permanent Refreshing Nectar stack.
3. Place the **Plastic Planter** in the **Blue Flower Field** for quick cycles of basic blue materials.

### Red Hive Farming Route
Red hives rely heavily on Invigorating and Motivating Nectars to maximize red pollen bursts and bee attack power.
1. Place the **Red Balloon Planter** in the **Pepper Patch** to gather Invigorating Nectar.
2. Place the **Heat-Treated Planter** in the **Rose Field** to secure high yields of red extracts and nectar.
3. Place the **Candy Planter** in the **Mushroom Field** to quickly farm Motivating Nectar.

<Callout type="warning" title="Over-Farming Fields">
Avoid placing the same planter in the same field repeatedly within a 24-hour window. Doing so triggers a diminishing returns penalty, drastically reducing the quality and quantity of the bonus loot drops.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Planter Farming Process">

Maximizing your planter efficiency requires a structured approach to planting, active harvesting, and nectar storage. Follow these steps to set up a highly productive daily routine.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip and Deploy">
    Select the appropriate planter from your inventory and stand in its preferred field. Click the planter icon to deploy it. Ensure you are matching the planter type to the field's preferred nectar type.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Boost Growth Rates">
    Spend at least 5 to 10 minutes actively collecting pollen in the field where your planter is deployed. Your bees gathering pollen in that field will accelerate the planter's growth timer significantly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Monitor Maturity">
    Check the growth percentage of your planter periodically. High-tier planters take longer to mature but can be left passively while you work on quests or farm other areas of the mountain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Harvest and Claim">
    Once the planter reaches 100% maturity, stand directly over it and click to harvest. Collect all the dropped tokens immediately, paying close attention to any rare crafting ingredients like glue, wax, or glitter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Condense and Store">
    Travel to the Nectar Condenser in the 30-Bee Zone. Deposit your newly harvested nectars to extend your active buff durations, ensuring your multipliers remain active for your next major boosting session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Active Farming Bonus">
Farming with active field boosts (such as those obtained from field boosters or glitter) while a planter is growing in that field will multiply the growth speed even further. Use this tactic to quickly cycle low-tier planters.
</Callout>

</Section>
