<Section icon={Map} title="Early Game: Starter Bear Questlines">

Bee swarm simulator quests are the backbone of your journey from a novice beekeeper to a hive master. In the early stages, your primary goal is to establish a consistent loop of collecting pollen and converting it into honey. The starter bears—Black Bear and Mother Bear—are designed to teach you these mechanics while providing the resources needed to hatch your first ten to twenty bees.

Black Bear offers a linear progression path that rewards you with Silver, Gold, and eventually Diamond or Mythic eggs. His tasks usually involve gathering specific amounts of pollen from fields like the Sunflower Patch, Mushroom Field, and Blue Flower Field. Mother Bear, on the other hand, focuses on "Bee Bond." Her quests require you to feed treats to your bees, which increases their level and combat effectiveness.

| Bear NPC | Unlock Requirement | Primary Reward Type | Strategic Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black Bear | Starter | Honey, Tickets, Eggs | Core progression and early wealth |
| Mother Bear | Starter | Treats, Star Treats | Essential for leveling and gifting bees |
| Brown Bear | Starter | Tickets, Consumables | Repeatable tasks for long-term gains |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Do not ignore Mother Bear's early tasks. Leveling your bees even by one or two levels significantly increases their gathering speed and attack power, making later combat-based quests much easier to handle.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Mid-Game Specialization and System Unlocks">

As you reach the 10-bee and 20-bee gates, **bee swarm simulator quests** become more complex, requiring you to interact with deeper game systems. Science Bear is the most critical NPC during this phase. His quests don't just give honey; they unlock "Translators," which allow you to communicate with Riley Bee, Bucko Bee, and Stick Bug.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Science Bear's Translators">
    Complete Science Bear's "Science Missions" to earn up to three Translators. Use the first one on either Riley Bee (Red) or Bucko Bee (Blue) depending on your preferred hive direction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Panda Bear's Combat Training">
    Engage in Panda Bear's quests to improve your combat skills. You will need to defeat Ladybugs, Rhinos, and Spiders. These quests eventually reward you with powerful Star Jellies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Badge Collection">
    Many mid-game quests require specific badges (e.g., Master or Grandmaster). Focus on farming one field consistently to hit these milestones while completing active tasks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| System Unlock | Required Bear | Key Prerequisite | Reward Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Red/Blue NPC Chat | Science Bear | 10+ Science Quests | Unlock specialized Red/Blue quests |
| Ant Challenge | Panda Bear | Combat Quests | Access to Ant Amulets for stat boosts |
| Stick Bug Challenge | Science Bear | Translator Item | High-tier rewards and specialized badges |

<Callout type="info" title="Translator Strategy">
Most players recommend using your first Science Bear Translator on Riley Bee or Bucko Bee to start earning Red or Blue extracts early. Save the final Translator for Stick Bug once your hive is strong enough for group challenges.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Endgame: The Spirit Bear Route">

However, the rewards—Spirit Petals—are mandatory for crafting the Petal Wand, Petal Belt, and summoning Windy Bee at the Wind Shrine.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Repeatable Quests and Challenge Modes">

Beyond the main story bears, **bee swarm simulator quests** include repeatable cycles that provide steady income and unique materials. Brown Bear and Robo Bear offer challenges that scale in difficulty as you complete more rounds.

| Challenge Type | NPC Host | Primary Goal | Scaling Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily Cycles | Brown Bear | Field specific pollen | Tickets and occasional Silver/Gold eggs |
| Robo Challenge | Robo Bear | Timed wave defense | Cogs, Drives, and Digital Bee progress |
| Ant Challenge | Panda Bear | Defeat waves of ants | Ant Amulets with critical hit/pollen buffs |

Robo Bear is particularly important for late-game players looking to optimize their hive with the Digital Bee. This questline involves timed challenges where you must collect specific tokens or pollen while fighting mechanical enemies like Mechsquitos and Cogmowers.

<Callout type="warning" title="Challenge Warning">
The Robo Bear Challenge is extremely resource-heavy. Do not attempt high-level rounds unless you have a surplus of Cogs and high-level bees (Level 12+), as the difficulty spikes sharply after round 5.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Seasonal and Event Questlines">

Seasonal events, most notably "Beesmas," introduce limited-time **bee swarm simulator quests** that offer some of the best rewards in the game. Bee Bear usually arrives during the winter season to provide a series of festive tasks. These quests reward players with Gingerbread Bears, Snowflakes, and Beequips—items that can be equipped to individual bees to boost their stats.

| Event Item | Source | Usage | Permanent? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gingerbread Bear | Bee Bear Quests | Spending in the Event Catalog | No (Expires) |
| Beequips | Seasonal Rewards | Stat boosts for specific bees | Yes (Retained) |
| Ornaments | NPC Event Quests | Permanent map-wide buffs | Yes (Retained) |

During these events, players should prioritize "Presents." You can deliver presents to various NPCs after completing their specific event quest, which grants massive honey boosts and rare items like Mythic Eggs or Star Jellies.

<Callout type="info" title="Event Timing">
Always check the official Onett Twitter or the Bee Swarm Discord for event end dates. Seasonal rewards are not permanent background furniture; once the event ends, the catalog closes, and unspent seasonal currency may vanish.
</Callout>
