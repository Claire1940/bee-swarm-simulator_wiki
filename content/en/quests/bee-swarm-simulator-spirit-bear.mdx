<Section icon={Target} title="Quest Progression and Milestones">

The quest line consists of 30 distinct tasks, divided into three blocks of 10. Each block increases in difficulty, requiring players to collect billions of pollen, share thousands of tokens, and defeat high-level field monsters. Completing the 10th, 20th, and 30th quests rewards the player with a Spirit Petal.

| Quest Block | Quest Range | Key Quest Milestone | Primary Reward |
| Level 1 | Quests 1 to 10 | Sway Into Spring (Quest 10) | First Spirit Petal |
| Level 2 | Quests 11 to 20 | Tickle the Wind (Quest 20) | Second Spirit Petal |
| Level 3 | Quests 21 to 30 | Spirit Journey (Quest 30) | Third Spirit Petal |

Because these quests require specific field pollen, players should plan their boosts accordingly. Farming without active boosts or field winds can make these quests take significantly longer.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Questing Strategy">

Farming efficiently for Spirit Bear requires utilizing game mechanics like field boosts, glues, and enzymes. Follow these steps to optimize your quest completion speed.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stockpile Materials Early">
    Before reaching the 30-Bee Zone, save your Glitter, Star Jellies, Enzymes, and Glue. Avoid using these materials for minor upgrades, as they are heavily required for crafting the Petal Wand and Petal Belt.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Wind Shrine Boosts">
    Before starting a major farming session, donate items to the Wind Shrine to gain field winds. This multiplies your pollen gathering rate in the specific fields required by your active Spirit Bear quest.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Coordinate with Sprout Parties">
    Many quests require token collection and massive pollen amounts. Coordinate with other players to pop sprouts in target fields, which spawns high-value tokens and boosts pollen generation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Hive Composition">
    Maintain a balanced hive of red, blue, and white bees until you complete all 30 quests. Switching to a single-color hive too early makes completing quests in opposing fields extremely difficult.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Boost Coordination">
Always check your active quest requirements before using field boosters in the Blue Flower, Red Flower, or Mountain Top dispensers to avoid wasting daily cooldowns.
</Callout>

</Section>
