To obtain **bee swarm simulator invigorating nectar**, you must place planters in specific fields. While many fields provide nectar, only a select few are designated as "Invigorating" sources. Farming in the wrong field will result in different nectar types, such as Comforting or Motivating, which may not align with your current Red-hive build or quest requirements.

The following fields are the only verified locations where planters will generate Invigorating Nectar:

| Field Name | Accessibility | Recommended Planter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clover Field** | Early Game (Any) | Paper / Plastic Planter |
| **Cactus Field** | Mid Game (15 Bees) | Tacky / Candy Planter |
| **Mountain Top Field** | Mid Game (25 Bees) | Red Clay / Pesticide Planter |
| **Pepper Field** | End Game (35 Bees) | Heat-Treated / Plenty Planter |

<Callout type="info" title="The Ant Field Glitch">
Onett previously announced that the Ant Field contains Invigorating Nectar. However, due to a long-standing mechanic quirk, harvesting here often grants Comforting Nectar instead. It is safer to stick to the Pepper or Mountain Top fields for consistent results.
</Callout>

Each field has a different "sip rate," which determines how quickly your bees gather nectar from the planter while you are actively farming. Staying in the field while the planter grows is the most efficient way to maximize your nectar gain per hour of real-time play.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Storage and Nectar Management">

Managing your **bee swarm simulator invigorating nectar** is just as important as farming it. Because nectar depletes even when you are offline, you must use the specialized tools located in the 30 and 35 Bee Zones to preserve your hard-earned buffs.

The **Nectar Pot**, located in the 30 Bee Zone, allows you to store your nectar for a cost of 5 Tickets. This "freezes" the nectar, preventing it from depleting. However, you must withdraw all stored nectar before you can deposit a different type.

The **Nectar Condenser**, found in the 35 Bee Zone, is the most efficient way to manage long-term stacks. It allows you to convert 12 hours of any nectar into a **Nectar Vial**.

| Item/Object | Function | Cost / Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nectar Vial** | Grants 1h 12m of Nectar | 12h Nectar + Condenser |
| **Nectar Pot** | Stores/Freezes Nectar | 5 Tickets per deposit |
| **Dapper Bear's Samovar** | Grants random Nectar | Beesmas Event Only |
| **Refreshing Vial** | Instant Nectar boost | Quest Reward / Crafting |

<Callout type="tip" title="Vial Strategy">
Always condense your nectar into Vials if you have more than 18 hours. This allows you to "burst" your buffs back to 24 hours instantly before a major boost session or a difficult boss fight.
</Callout>
