<Section icon={Sparkles} title="What Are Stingers & Why Do You Need Them?">

In Bee Swarm Simulator, progress is measured by the strength of your hive and the quality of your gear. Among the various crafting materials you will encounter, **bee swarm simulator stingers** stand out as one of the most valuable resources. These sharp, neon-blue items serve a dual purpose: they function as a powerful temporary combat consumable and acts as the primary currency for some of the most influential upgrades in the game. 

When consumed directly from your inventory, a single stinger boosts the attack power of your entire bee swarm by 50% for 30 seconds. While this is incredibly useful for taking down tough bosses like the Coconut Crab or the Commando Chick, hoarding them for crafting is almost always the superior long-term strategy. 

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management Warning">
Avoid consuming stingers casually during early-game farming. Saving stingers for major milestones like the Vicious Bee Egg and the Demon Mask will accelerate your overall progression far more than temporary combat boosts.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Farm Bee Swarm Simulator Stingers">

Farming **bee swarm simulator stingers** efficiently requires a deep understanding of the game's day and night cycles. The primary source of stingers is the Wild Vicious Bee, an aggressive rogue mob that only spawns during the night. A standard day-night cycle in Bee Swarm Simulator lasts 20 minutes: 16 minutes of daylight followed by 4 minutes of darkness.

When night falls, a Wild Vicious Bee has a chance to spawn in one of several designated fields. You can identify its presence by looking for a distinct, silver-blue metallic spike protruding from the field's clover or flowers. Walking directly into this spike will trigger the Vicious Bee to emerge, initiating a combat encounter.

<Callout type="tip" title="Spotting the Spike">
Turn down your graphics settings slightly if you are having trouble seeing through dense flower fields. This reduces the visual clutter of the grass, making the metallic blue spike of the Vicious Bee stand out clearly.
</Callout>

### Best Fields for Wild Vicious Bee Spawns

The level of the Wild Vicious Bee, as well as the number of stingers it drops upon defeat, scales with the field in which it spawns. Higher-level fields spawn tougher bees but reward you with significantly more stingers.

| Field Name | Spawn Tier | Level Range | Spawn Chance | Strategy Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mountain Top Field** | High | 8 - 12 | Moderate | Look near the back wall and around the edges |
| **Rose Field** | High | 6 - 10 | Moderate | Red flower cover makes the spike hard to see |
| **Cactus Field** | Medium | 5 - 8 | High | Easiest field to spot the spike quickly |
| **Spider Field** | Medium | 4 - 7 | Moderate | Watch out for the spider mob spawning simultaneously |
| **Clover Field** | Low | 2 - 5 | Low | Great for lower-level players during early night |

### Step-by-Step Nighttime Farming Routine

Follow this optimized rotation at the start of every night cycle to maximize your chances of finding a Wild Vicious Bee.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor the Sky">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check the Lower Fields First">
    Start by scanning the Clover Field and Spider Field. These are close together and can be checked within seconds. Look for the sharp silver spike.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ascend to the 15-Bee Zone">

  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check the Mountain Top">

  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Engage and Defeat">

  </Step>

<Section icon={Target} title="Alternative Stinger Sources & Dispensers">

While hunting Wild Vicious Bees is the most reliable active farming method, relying solely on night cycles can be slow. Fortunately, there are several alternative methods to supplement your stinger income, including daily rewards, challenges, and specialized dispensers.

The most notable static source is the Stinger Dispenser located near the Cactus Field. This machine requires you to have unlocked at least 25 bees to access. While convenient, it comes with a steep trade-off that players must consider before using it regularly.

<Callout type="warning" title="Dispenser Cost Efficiency">
The Stinger Dispenser charges 10 Tickets per use and has a 1-hour cooldown. This is generally considered a poor deal for early and mid-game players, as Tickets are better spent on Event Bee Eggs (such as Tabby or Photon Bee). Only use the dispenser if you are a few stingers short of a major upgrade.
</Callout>
